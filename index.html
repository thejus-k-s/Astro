Who We Are

Humanity's Curiosity
Engineered Here

A student-led astronomy collective at the College of Engineering Trivandrum, Kerala.

AstroCET is a community of passionate stargazers, engineers, and dreamers at CET who believe the sky is not a limit — it's a beginning. We bring together students across all departments to study, observe, and celebrate the universe.

From late-night observation camps at the darkest sites in Kerala to cutting-edge astrophotography workshops and ISRO-inspired rocket design competitions — AstroCET exists to ignite the astronomer in every engineer.

🔭
Observation
Regular sky-watch sessions with telescopes and star charts.
📸
Astrophotography
Capture nebulae, planets and cosmic deep-field objects.
🚀
Rocketry
Design, build and launch model rockets with real propulsion.
🌌
Research
Papers, simulations and ISRO outreach collaborations.

Deep Space

Objects We Chase

Our target list this season — from nearby planets to the edges of the observable universe.

🪐
Saturn's Ring System
Currently at opposition — the best time of year to observe the ringed giant through our 12" Dobsonian. Ring tilt at 27° makes the Cassini Division visible.
Currently Visible
🌌
Andromeda Galaxy
M31, our nearest galactic neighbor at 2.5 million light-years. Autumn brings it to zenith. We're attempting our first long-exposure mosaic this season.
Astrophotography Target
💥
Orion Nebula
The Sword of Orion hides one of the most spectacular stellar nurseries in our galaxy. M42 is our go-to for demonstrating telescopic power to newcomers.
Beginner Friendly
⭐
Perseids Meteor Shower
Peak August 12-13. Up to 100 meteors per hour from the debris trail of Comet Swift–Tuttle. Our annual dark-sky camp destination: Munnar highlands.
Annual Event
🌙
Lunar Terminator
The line between light and dark on the Moon is where craters and mountain ranges cast the longest shadows. Best viewed 3-4 days past new moon.
Monthly Session
🔴
Mars Close Approach
The Red Planet reaches opposition in January 2026. AstroCET is planning a 3-night observation marathon tracking polar ice caps and dust storms.
Upcoming 2026
